### Day 3-4: Business Entity Formation ‚è±Ô∏è 2-3 hours

**Why:** You need a legal business entity to open a bank account, get licenses, and protect your personal assets.

#### Task 1: Choose Entity Type

- [ ] **Decide: LLC or Corporation?** (30 minutes)
  
  **Recommended: LLC (Limited Liability Company)**
  
  **Why LLC?**
  - ‚úÖ Limited liability protection (your personal assets are safe)
  - ‚úÖ Pass-through taxation (no double taxation)
  - ‚úÖ Easy to convert to Corp later if you raise VC funding
  - ‚úÖ Lower cost ($100-$800 to form)
  - ‚úÖ Less paperwork than a corporation
  
  **When to choose Corporation instead:**
  - You're raising VC funding immediately
  - You want to issue stock options to employees
  - You plan to go public someday
  
  **Our Recommendation:** Start with LLC, convert to Corp when you raise Series A funding.

#### Task 2: Choose State of Incorporation

- [ ] **Decide where to form your business** (15 minutes)
  
  **Option A: Your Home State** ‚úÖ **RECOMMENDED FOR STARTUP**
  - Easier and cheaper
  - You understand local requirements
  - Faster processing
  - Can always reincorporate later
  
  **Option B: Delaware**
  - Best for VC funding
  - Well-established corporate law
  - More expensive ($500-$2,000)
  - More paperwork
  
  **Our Recommendation:** Start in your home state. You can always reincorporate in Delaware when you raise Series A funding.

#### Task 3: Reserve Business Name

- [ ] **Check name availability** (30 minutes)
  - Go to your state's Secretary of State website
  - Search for "Shot On Me" (or your preferred name)
  - Make sure it's available
  
- [ ] **Check domain availability** (15 minutes)
  - Check if shotonme.com is available
  - Or find an alternative domain
  - **Tip:** Even if you don't buy it now, check availability
  
- [ ] **Reserve the name** (15 minutes)
  - File name reservation with your state
  - Cost: $10-$50
  - Valid for 30-120 days (varies by state)

**üí° Pro Tip:** If your preferred name is taken, try variations like "ShotOnMe", "Shot On Me App", or "ShotOnMePay"

**‚úÖ Week 1, Day 3-4 Complete When:**
- You've decided on LLC or Corp
- You've chosen your state of incorporation
- You've reserved your business name

### Day 5-7: File Formation Documents ‚è±Ô∏è 3-4 hours

**Why:** This makes your business legally official. You can't open a bank account or get licenses without this.

#### Task 1: File Articles of Organization (LLC)

- [ ] **Gather required information** (30 minutes)
  - Business name
  - Business address (can be your home initially)
  - Registered agent (you can be your own, or use a service)
  - Purpose of business
  
- [ ] **File online** (1 hour)
  - Go to your state's Secretary of State website
  - Find "Form a Business" or "File Articles of Organization"
  - Fill out the form online
  - Pay filing fee: $100-$800 (varies by state)
  
- [ ] **Wait for approval** (1-4 weeks)
  - Most states process within 1-2 weeks
  - You'll receive confirmation by email
  - Save this document - you'll need it for everything!

**üí° Pro Tip:** Some states offer expedited processing for an extra fee ($50-$200). If you're in a hurry, it's worth it.

#### Task 2: Get EIN (Employer Identification Number)

- [ ] **Apply online** (30 minutes)
  - Go to: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-ein-online
  - **FREE** - No cost!
  - Fill out the form (takes about 15 minutes)
  - You'll get your EIN immediately
  
- [ ] **Save your EIN confirmation** (5 minutes)
  - Download the PDF confirmation
  - Save it in your business folder
  - You'll need this for bank account, taxes, everything

**Why EIN?**
- Required to open a business bank account
- Required for tax filing
- Required if you hire employees
- It's like a Social Security Number for your business

#### Task 3: Create Operating Agreement

- [ ] **Get a template** (30 minutes)
  - LegalZoom: https://www.legalzoom.com
  - Rocket Lawyer: https://www.rocketlawyer.com
  - Or ask your attorney for a template
  
- [ ] **Fill out the template** (1 hour)
  - Include ownership percentages
  - Management structure
  - Decision-making process
  - What happens if a founder leaves
  
- [ ] **Have attorney review** (optional but recommended)
  - Cost: $200-$500
  - They'll make sure it's legally sound
  - **Tip:** If you're a solo founder, you can skip attorney review initially

**‚úÖ Week 1, Day 5-7 Complete When:**
- Articles of Organization filed
- EIN obtained and saved
- Operating Agreement drafted

- [ ] **Set up online banking** (30 minutes)

### Day 11-12: Stripe Business Account ‚è±Ô∏è 2-3 hours

**Why:** Stripe processes your payments. You need a business account (not test mode) to accept real money.

#### Task 1: Upgrade Stripe to Business Account

- [ ] **Log into Stripe Dashboard** (15 minutes)
  - Go to: https://dashboard.stripe.com
  - You should already have a test account
  
- [ ] **Complete business verification** (1-2 hours)
  - Provide your EIN
  - Business address
  - Business type (LLC)
  - Link your business bank account
  - Complete identity verification (they'll ask for ID)
  
- [ ] **Wait for approval** (1-3 days)
  - Stripe will review your application
  - They may ask for additional documents
  - Once approved, you can accept real payments!

**üí° Pro Tip:** Stripe's approval is usually fast (1-3 days), but have all documents ready to speed it up.

#### Task 2: Understand Stripe Fees

- [ ] **Learn about Stripe's fee structure** (30 minutes)
  - Standard: 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction
  - Example: $50 transaction = $1.75 fee
  - No monthly fees, no setup fees
  
- [ ] **Decide: Will you charge users fees?** (30 minutes)
  
  **Option A: Pass fees to users** ‚úÖ **RECOMMENDED**
  - User pays: $50 + $1.75 fee = $51.75
  - You receive: $50
  - **Pro:** No cost to you
  - **Con:** Slightly higher cost for users
  
  **Option B: Absorb fees yourself**
  - User pays: $50
  - You receive: $48.25 (after Stripe fee)
  - **Pro:** Lower cost for users
  - **Con:** Cost to you
  
  **Our Recommendation:** Pass fees to users (industry standard). Most payment apps do this.

### Day 13-14: Financial Projections ‚è±Ô∏è 3-4 hours

  - Transaction fees (2.5-2.9% of transaction volume)
  - Venue commissions (5-10% of redemptions)
  - Premium subscriptions ($4.99/month per user)
  
  **Example Calculation:**
  ```
  Month 1: 100 users √ó $25 avg transaction √ó 10 transactions = $25,000 volume
  Transaction fees: $25,000 √ó 2.5% = $625/month
  Venue commissions: $5,000 redemptions √ó 7% = $350/month
  Total Revenue: $975/month
  ```
  
  **Conservative Growth:**
  - Month 1-3: $500-$2,500/month
  - Month 4-6: $2,500-$5,000/month
  - Month 7-9: $5,000-$15,000/month
  - Month 10-12: $15,000-$50,000/month
  
- [ ] **Project costs** (1 hour)
  
  **Fixed Costs (Monthly):**
  - Legal/compliance: $2,000
  - Insurance: $500-$2,000
  - Hosting: $100-$200
  - Software tools: $200-$500
  - Salaries: $5,000-$15,000
  - Marketing: $1,000-$5,000
  - **Total: $9,000-$25,000/month**
  
  **Variable Costs:
  - Stripe fees: 2.9% + $0.30 (passed to users)
  - SMS (Twilio): $0.0075 per message
  - Support: $0.50-$2.00 per user (if outsourced)
  
- [ ] **Calculate break-even** (30 minutes)
  - Find the month where revenue > costs
  - Most businesses break even in Month 9-12

- [ ] **Calculate initial investment** (30 minutes)

  - Legal setup: $15,000-$25,000

  - Marketing (beta): $5,000-$10,000
  - **Total: $50,000-$100,000**
  
- [ ] **Decide funding strategy** (30 minutes)
  
  **Option A: Bootstrap** ‚úÖ **RECOMMENDED FOR MVP**
  - Use your own money
  - Validate product first
  - Raise funds later when you have traction

  - Raise $25,000-$50,000
  - Usually convertible notes or equity
  - Faster than professional investors

  - Raise $100,000-$500,000

- [ ] **Choose your approach** (30 minutes)

  - Cost: $2,000-$5,000
  - They'll draft everything
  - Legally sound and customized
  - **Best for:** Serious launch, have budget
  
  **Option B: Template + Attorney Review**
  - Get template from LegalZoom or Rocket Lawyer

  - Have attorney review: $500-$1,000
  - **Best for:** Budget-conscious, still want legal review
  
  **Option C: Template Only** ‚ö†Ô∏è **NOT RECOMMENDED**

  - No legal review
  - **Risk:** May not cover your specific business model
  
- [ ] **What must be included** (attorney will handle this, but good to know):
  - User agreement (age restrictions, account creation)

  - **User rights:**

    - Right to delete their data
    - Right to opt-out of marketing

  - **Security measures:**
    - Encryption
    - Secure servers
    - Access controls
  
- [ ] **GDPR Compliance** (if serving EU users)
  - Cookie consent banner
  - Data export functionality
  - Right to deletion
  - Data portability
  
- [ ] **CCPA Compliance** (if serving CA users)
  - "Do Not Sell My Data" option
  - Data deletion requests
  - Opt-out mechanisms

### Day 18-19: FinCEN Registration ‚è±Ô∏è 2-3 hours

- [ ] **Submit the form** (15 minutes)
  - Review everything carefully
  - Submit online
  - You'll get a confirmation email
  
- [ ] **Wait for approval** (30-60 days)
  - FinCEN will review your application
  - They may contact you with questions
  - Once approved, you're officially registered!

**üí° Pro Tip:** You can register BEFORE you start operations, but you MUST register within 180 days of starting. Do it early!

### Day 22-24: AML Compliance Program ‚è±Ô∏è 3-4 hours

**Why:** Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance is required by law. You need a written program to prevent money laundering and fraud.

#### Task 1: Develop AML Program

- [ ] **Create written AML policy** (2-3 hours)
  
  **What to include:**
  - **Customer Identification Program (CIP):**
    - How you verify user identity
    - What documents you require
    - When to flag suspicious activity
  
  - **Transaction Monitoring:**
    - How you monitor transactions
    - What patterns are suspicious
    - When to file Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
  
  - **Record Keeping:**
    - What records to keep
    - How long to keep them (5 years minimum)
    - Where to store them securely
  
  - **Training Program:**
    - How you'll train staff on AML
    - Annual training requirements
    - How to recognize suspicious activity
  
- [ ] **Get attorney review** (optional but recommended)
  - Cost: $500-$1,000
  - They'll make sure it's legally sound
  - **Tip:** Many attorneys have AML program templates
