# Lean Launch Strategy - Launching Shot On Me with Minimal Investment

**Goal:** Launch legally with $10,000-$25,000 instead of $50,000-$100,000

---

## The Reality Check

**Critical Fact:** You **CANNOT** legally accept real payments without money transmitter licenses. Operating without licenses = fines, shutdown, potential criminal penalties.

**BUT** - You can launch in phases with a much smaller initial investment!

---

## Phase 1: Minimum Viable Launch ($10,000-$15,000)

### What You CAN Do Legally:

✅ **Launch in ONE state only** (your home state or most business-friendly state)  
✅ **Start with limited features** (validate product-market fit)  
✅ **Use Stripe's licenses** (they handle payment processing compliance)  
✅ **Bootstrap everything** (founders work for equity, minimal salaries)

### Phase 1 Investment Breakdown:

#### 1. Legal Foundation: $3,000-$5,000
- **Entity Formation (LLC):** $500-$1,000
  - Form in your home state (cheaper than Delaware)
  - Can reincorporate later if needed
- **Basic Terms of Service:** $1,500-$3,000
  - Use template + attorney review (not full custom drafting)
  - Can enhance later
- **Basic Privacy Policy:** $1,000-$2,000
  - Template + attorney review
- **Attorney Consultation (2-3 hours):** $500-$1,000
  - Get legal advice on minimum requirements
  - Understand your specific state's requirements

**Savings vs. Full Legal:** $12,000-$20,000

#### 2. ONE State License: $5,000-$10,000
- **Choose the cheapest/easiest state:**
  - **Texas:** $1,000-$5,000 application, $100K net worth, $50K-$500K bond
  - **Wyoming:** Very business-friendly, lower costs
  - **Your Home State:** Usually easiest (you're already there)
- **Application Fee:** $1,000-$5,000
- **Bond Cost (annual):** $500-$2,000 (1-3% of bond amount)
- **Compliance Documentation:** $2,000-$3,000
  - Basic AML program
  - KYC procedures
  - Can use templates + attorney review

**Savings vs. Multiple States:** $15,000-$20,000

#### 3. Basic Insurance: $2,000-$3,500
- **General Liability:** $500-$1,000/year
- **Cyber Liability:** $1,000-$2,000/year
- **Skip E&O and D&O initially** (add later when you have revenue)

**Savings vs. Full Insurance:** $4,500-$18,500

#### 4. Security/Compliance: $1,000-$2,000
- **Basic Security Review:** $1,000-$2,000
  - Use automated tools (free/cheap)
  - Get developer friend to review (free)
  - Professional audit later when you have revenue
- **PCI DSS:** $0 (Stripe handles this)
- **AML/KYC Program:** $500-$1,000
  - Use templates, basic implementation
  - Enhance later

**Savings vs. Full Security:** $4,000-$8,000

#### 5. Operating Expenses (1 Month): $2,000-$4,000
- **Legal/Compliance:** $500 (minimal ongoing)
- **Hosting:** $100-$200
- **Software Tools:** $200-$500
- **No Salaries** (founders work for equity)
- **Minimal Marketing:** $1,000-$2,000
  - Organic social media only
  - No paid ads initially

**Savings vs. 3 Months:** $8,000-$16,000

#### 6. Marketing: $1,000-$2,000
- **Organic Only:**
  - Social media (free)
  - Word of mouth
  - Friends & family
  - Local networking
- **No paid ads, influencers, or PR initially**

**Savings vs. Full Marketing:** $4,000-$8,000

---

## Phase 1 Total: $10,000-$15,000

**What You Get:**
- ✅ Legally compliant in ONE state
- ✅ Can accept real payments

- ✅ 1 month operating runway
- ✅ Ability to validate product-market fit

## Alternative: Partner with Licensed Provider

### Option: Use a Licensed Money Transmitter

**How It Works:**
- Partner with existing licensed money transmitter
- They handle compliance, you pay fees
- You focus on product and user experience

**Costs:**
- Setup: $5,000-$10,000 (partnership agreement, integration)
- Ongoing: 0.5-1% of transaction volume (vs. 2.5-2.9% you charge)
- **Total Initial Investment: $5,000-$10,000**

**Pros:**
- ✅ Much lower upfront cost
- ✅ Faster to launch (weeks vs. months)
- ✅ No state license applications
- ✅ They handle compliance

**Cons:**
- ❌ Less control
- ❌ Higher ongoing fees
- ❌ Dependent on partner
- ❌ Lower profit margins

**Best For:** Launching ASAP with minimal capital

## Risk Assessment: Launching with $10K-$15K

### Legal Risks: LOW ✅
- You're fully compliant in your launch state
- Operating legally
- Can expand as revenue grows

### Financial Risks: MEDIUM ⚠️
- 1 month runway is tight
- Need revenue quickly
- No safety net

### Operational Risks: MEDIUM ⚠️
- Minimal marketing = slower growth
- No salaries = founders must have other income
- Basic insurance = less protection

### Mitigation Strategies:
1. **Have 3-6 months personal savings** (for living expenses)
2. **Keep day job initially** (if possible)
3. **Focus on organic growth** (word of mouth, social media)
4. **Get first 100 users quickly** (friends, family, local network)
5. **Reinvest ALL revenue** (back into business for first 6 months)

## Recommended Approach: Hybrid Lean Launch

### Month 1: Legal Foundation ($10,000-$15,000)
- Form LLC
- Get ONE state license (cheapest/easiest)
- Basic ToS/Privacy Policy
- Basic insurance
- Launch in one state only

### Month 2-3: Revenue Generation
- Focus on acquiring first 100-500 users
- Organic marketing only
- Reinvest ALL revenue back into business
- Target: $2,000-$5,000/month revenue

### Month 4-6: Revenue-Funded Growth
- Use revenue to add second state ($5,000-$10,000)
- Enhance legal documents ($2,000-$5,000)
- Add marketing budget ($2,000-$5,000/month)
- Target: $10,000-$15,000/month revenue

### Month 7-12: Scale
- Add more states as revenue allows
- Full insurance coverage
- Professional security audit
- Founder salaries
- Target: $25,000-$50,000/month revenue
