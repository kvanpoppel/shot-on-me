## TOPICS TO ADDRESS

### 1. Business Structure & Entity Formation

**Key Questions:**
- Should I form an LLC, Corporation, or other entity?
- What state should I incorporate in? (Indiana for Indiana operations)
- What are the tax implications of each structure?
- Do I need to form the entity before applying for license?
- What are the ongoing compliance requirements?

**What to Discuss:**
- Your current business structure (if any)
- Whether you need to form entity before license application
- Tax considerations (pass-through vs. corporate)
- Liability protection needs
- Future expansion plans

---

### 2. Money Transmitter License - Indiana

**Key Questions:**
- What are the exact requirements for Indiana money transmitter license?
- What is the application process and timeline?
- What are the exact costs (application fee, bond, net worth)?
- What documents do I need to prepare?
- What is the bond requirement and how do I obtain it?
- What are the net worth requirements?
- What ongoing reporting is required?
- How long does the application process take?

**What to Discuss:**
- Your business model (escrow + virtual cards)
- Whether your model qualifies as money transmission
- Any exemptions or exceptions that might apply
- Application timeline and milestones
- Required documentation

---

### 3. Federal Requirements (FinCEN)

**Key Questions:**
- Do I need to register with FinCEN as a Money Services Business (MSB)?
- What is the registration process?
- What are the ongoing requirements?
- What are the penalties for non-compliance?

**What to Discuss:**
- FinCEN registration requirements
- MSB registration process
- Ongoing reporting obligations
- Relationship between federal and state requirements

## WHAT TO EXPECT DURING THE MEETING

### 1. Initial Discussion (15-30 minutes)

**Attorney Will Likely:**
- Ask about your business model
- Understand your transaction flow
- Identify key legal issues
- Assess your needs
- Explain their services

**You Should:**
- Provide clear overview of your business
- Ask questions about their experience
- Understand their fee structure
- Get a sense of their communication style

### 2. Detailed Analysis (30-60 minutes)

**Attorney Will Likely:**
- Analyze your business model
- Identify specific legal requirements
- Discuss compliance needs
- Explain licensing process
- Outline next steps

**You Should:**
- Take detailed notes
- Ask clarifying questions
- Understand the requirements
- Get cost estimates
- Understand timeline

### 3. Q&A Session (15-30 minutes)

**Attorney Will Likely:**
- Answer your specific questions
- Address concerns
- Provide recommendations
- Discuss alternatives

**You Should:**
- Ask all your questions
- Get clarification on unclear points
- Understand recommendations
- Discuss next steps

## RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

### About the Attorney

**Warning Signs:**
- ❌ Attorney has no experience with money transmitter licensing
- ❌ Attorney is vague about costs or timeline
- ❌ Attorney can't answer basic questions
- ❌ Attorney seems unfamiliar with your business model
- ❌ Attorney doesn't ask detailed questions about your business
- ❌ Attorney promises unrealistic timelines
- ❌ Attorney doesn't explain risks

**Good Signs:**
- ✅ Attorney has specific experience with money transmitter licensing
- ✅ Attorney asks detailed questions about your business
- ✅ Attorney explains risks and requirements clearly
- ✅ Attorney provides realistic timelines and costs
- ✅ Attorney references specific regulations
- ✅ Attorney discusses compliance requirements
- ✅ Attorney explains next steps clearly
